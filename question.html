I'm looking for some advice on how to feed a street dog that's quite aggressive. There's a stray in my neighborhood that I'd really like to help, but every time I get close, it shows signs of aggression such as growling and barking. I'm worried about its well-being and want to make sure it gets enough food and water, but I'm also concerned about my safety.

What are some safe and effective methods for approaching and feeding an aggressive street dog? Are there specific techniques or tools that can help me gain its trust over time? Any tips or experiences from those who have dealt with similar situations would be greatly appreciated.

Feeding an aggressive street dog requires caution and patience. Observe the dog from a distance to understand its behavior and place food and water at a safe distance, gradually decreasing it as the dog becomes accustomed to you. Avoid direct eye contact and move slowly. Offering high-value treats like cooked meat can help gain trust. Use a long-handled scoop or stick to push the food closer for safety. Consistent feeding times and locations help the dog see you as a reliable food source. Consulting a local animal rescue organization or professional behaviorist can provide additional guidance.
